Switzerland · August 7–17

River, Alps, Golden Train

A romantic, outdoorsy Switzerland trip with short “holy shit” hikes, the Aare float, a panoramic train day, Lake Geneva, and one last late night in Zurich.

ShapeCalendarHikesGoldenPassWhy this works

This is not “see Switzerland.”

This is a trip with a story: start in Bern with the turquoise Aare, settle into the Bernese Alps for four nights of manageable hikes, then ride the GoldenPass down to Lake Geneva before a proper Zurich finale.

It keeps the famous Swiss romance — trains, mountains, lake towns, old streets, outdoor cafés — without turning every day into a transfer day.

Four nights in Grindelwald is the point.

This is the Swiss version of a great national park base: you do not need long, brutal hikes to see amazing things. You use trains and lifts to reach the good stuff, then walk the beautiful part.

The GoldenPass is the elegant train day.

It belongs in this itinerary because it connects the mountains to Lake Geneva instead of dragging the whole trip to a far corner of the country.

  • Feels special without consuming multiple days.
  • Gives you the romantic panoramic train experience.
  • Leaves the Bernina Express for a future “Switzerland II” trip instead of cramming.
  • Pairs beautifully with a Vevey lakefront night.

It has variety without chaos.

  • Bern: culture, river, evening wandering.
  • Grindelwald: a real mountain base with multiple short-hike options.
  • GoldenPass: the “we came to Switzerland for trains” day.
  • Vevey: lakefront romance and wine country texture.
  • Zurich: one last night out before the flight.

A river you can float, mountains you can actually walk, a train day worth dressing up for, and a lakefront ending.
